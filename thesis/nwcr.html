North-West Corner Rule

The North-West Corner Rule is a technique calculating the first possible solution to a transportation problem.

This Approach is called North-West Corner because the fundamental variables are chosen from the left corner.

In North-West Corner Rule we begin with (A1, B1) cell allocate to C11 as many units as possible as without violating the constraints.

This will be the smallest of A1 & B1. therefore, we continue by moving oneself to the right of some supply remains or if not one cell down.

At each step, allocate as much as possible to the cell under consideration.

Steps North-West Corner Rule:

  1. Start on the upper left corner cell of the transportation matrix and allocate minimum (A1, B1)
    1. a.Subtract this value from supply and demand of respect row and column
    2. “If supply” is zero, then cross that row and move down to the next cell
    3. If “demand” is zero, then cross that column and move right to the next cell
    4. If “supply” and “demand” both are zero, then cross both row and column and move diagonally to the next cell
  3. Repeat the steps until all Supply and Demand values are 0.

(Note that the null row is added to make supply and demand values equal.)
