Least cost method

The Least Cost Method is a method that is used for determining the most cost-effective first solution to a transportation problem.

The allocation process starts with the cell having the lowest cost. With the goal of having the lowest transportation cost, the lower-cost cells are picked above the higher-cost cells.

The Least Cost Method is presumed to produce better results than the North-West Corner Method because it takes shipping costs into account when allocating resources, whereas the North-West Corner Method only considers availability and supply requirements and starts allocating resources from the extreme left corner, regardless of shipping costs.

In Least - Cost Method allocate min(Ai,Bj) to the cell having lowest cost in the matrix.

Ignore the satisfied row and column, carry on allocations on the same way for the remaining submatrix.

In case of tie, choose a cell which can accomadate max allocation or select arbitrary.

Steps for Least Cost Method:

  1. Choose the cell with the minimum unit cost, Cij, and allocate as much as possible, i.e., minimum (A1, B1)
    1. Take this minimum value and subtract it from supply Ai and demand Bi
    2. Cross that row if the supply Ai is 0, and cross that column if the demand Bi is 0.
    3. If the min unit cost cell is not unique, choose the cell with the highest possible allocation.
  3. Repeat the steps until all Supply and Demand values are 0.

(Note that the null row is added to make supply and demand values equal.)

remember, no allocation can be negative (-) but might be 0.
